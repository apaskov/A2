Assignment 2 Exploratory Data Analysis

Alex Paskov — apaskov@mit.edu

Subtheme: Housing for the Rich

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. Is the rising price of housing inflation-driven, or are there other factors at play?
  2. Are certain regions more prone to investor activity?
  3. How does the practice of cash purchasing affect the housing market?
  4. How does the practice of house flipping affect the housing market?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Through this exploratory data analysis, we search for insights into the housing market pricing explosion in Boston. Our first steps were to ensure that the dataset contained quality data and only the data relevant to our analysis, leading us to distill the transactions to a subset of zip codes and property types. We then began our exploration to address our four questions. We ultimately found that the rising price of housing is not solely driven by inflation, but also likely influenced by increased investor activity, cash purchasing, and house flipping. Now, we cannot definitively say that these factors are mostly responsible for the housing price explosion, so further analysis across a greater breadth of data should be done to answer these pressing issues affecting the average Bostonian.
